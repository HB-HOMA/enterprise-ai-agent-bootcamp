# Before/After: Voice Profile Impact (Day 3)

## Same email (from James), two different system prompts

### BEFORE (Day 2 system prompt — rules only, no voice profile)
Hi James,

Thank you for the clarification — I'll make sure to have the artwork
ready in one of those formats (AI, PSD, or EPS) and will send it over
shortly. I appreciate you forwarding the details to your team, and I
look forward to hearing back on the sizing and pricing. Please don't
hesitate to reach out if you need anything else in the meantime.

Best regards, Homa

### AFTER (Day 3 system prompt — rules + voice/tone context injection)
Thanks for the clarification, James — I'll get the artwork files over
to you in AI or EPS format shortly. Great to hear the team is already
working on the sizing and pricing; I'll keep an eye out for those
details. In the meantime, feel free to reach out if you need anything
else from my end.

Best regards,
Homa

## What changed and why
Both drafts are professional and follow every rule (length, sign-off,
no pricing commitments). The difference is in RHYTHM and WARMTH:
- "AFTER" opens with the sender's name naturally woven in, rather than
  a separate greeting line
- "AFTER" uses more natural connective phrasing ("Great to hear...")
  instead of more formal transitions ("I appreciate you forwarding...")
- "AFTER" closes more personally ("from my end") vs. more generic
  ("in the meantime")

## Key lesson
This demonstrates the real difference between a SYSTEM PROMPT (rules,
constraints -- what must never break) and CONTEXT INJECTION (a voice
profile -- how something should feel). Separating the two makes each
easier to maintain and test independently.
