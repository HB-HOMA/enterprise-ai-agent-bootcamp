# First Successful LinkedIn Post Draft (Day 4)

## Topic given to the agent
The lesson I learned building my first AI agent this week — that
automation is only as good as the human review step behind it.

## AI-drafted post (Claude, using linkedin-agent-system-prompt v1)
Building my first AI agent this week taught me something I didn't
expect to learn.

The agent worked. It ran the workflow, made decisions, produced
outputs — faster than I could have done it manually. But the moment I
skipped reviewing its work closely, things quietly went sideways. Not
dramatically wrong. Just... subtly off in ways that compound over time.

That's the part no one warns you about. Automation doesn't eliminate
judgment — it just moves the moment where judgment matters. The human
review step isn't a fallback for when AI fails. It's what makes the
whole thing actually reliable.

If you're building with AI agents right now, where are you placing
that review checkpoint in your workflow?
